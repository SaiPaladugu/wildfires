03

Research Question

Can we predict whether a wildfire was naturally or human-caused based on temporal, geographic, and climate features?

Binary classification: Natural (lightning) vs. Human (accidental, negligence, industrial)

04

Data Sources

CNFDB Fire Data

  • 374,037 fires with known cause
  • 1972–2024, all provinces
  • Location, date, size, cause label
  • 45.9% Natural / 54.1% Human

Environment Canada Climate

  • 231 weather stations across all provinces & territories
  • 12,243 daily climate CSV files
  • Temperature, precipitation, snow, wind
  • Matched to fires via haversine distance

08

Model: Gradient Boosted Decision Tree

Hyperparameters

  • 500 trees (early stopping, patience 20)
  • Learning rate: 0.05
  • Max depth: 5
  • Row subsample: 80%
  • Column subsample: √p

Design Choices

  • Class-weighted sampling for imbalance
  • Missing values encoded as −999
  • Stratified 80/20 train-test split
  • 5-fold cross-validation

Statistical Significance

5-Fold Cross-Validation

  • ROC-AUC: 0.932 ± 0.0003
  • PR-AUC: 0.945 ± 0.0002
  • F1: 0.863 ± 0.0009
  • Extremely low variance across folds

Significance Tests

  • Permutation test: p < 0.001 (1,000 iterations)
  • Bootstrap 95% CI:
  • ROC-AUC: [0.931, 0.934]
  • PR-AUC: [0.945, 0.948]
18

Implications

  • Targeted prevention: Focus campaigns on April–June when human fires peak
  • Regional prioritization: Eastern provinces (ON, QC) need awareness programs; northern territories need detection systems
  • Risk scoring: Well-calibrated probabilities (Brier 0.104) enable daily risk assessments
  • Investigation: Guide post-hoc cause investigation for unknown-cause fires
19

Limitations & Future Work

Limitations

  • Station coverage uneven (median 102 km, remote areas worse)
  • No FWI (Fire Weather Index) computed
  • Fire size is a post-ignition feature
  • No land-use or population data

Future Work

  • Integrate Canadian FWI system
  • Add road/population proximity features
  • Satellite vegetation data (NDVI)
  • Real-time operational deployment
